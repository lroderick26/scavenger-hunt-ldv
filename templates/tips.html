Digital Scavenger Hunt

Thank you so much for joining us for a digital scavenger hunt!

The purpose of the game is to find and upload some kind of photo or screenshot that meets the criteria of a given prompt in the allotted timeframe.

You may not already know the answers to the prompts, so go ahead and head to Google to do some research and find a representative photo.

If you have any questions, feel free to let us know via WebEx chat.

How to Play

Follow the steps below to get started:

  1. Go to the Prompts page and look through the prompts listed there.
  2. The idea of the game is to find a photo that represents the prompt, so once you've identified the prompt you'd like to find, go to Google and do some sleuthing.
  3. Once you've found a photo you like, save or screenshot the image and then head back to the Prompts page.
  4. On the Prompts page, click to Upload a Photo.
  5. Wait for a confirmation that the photo has been successfully uploaded, then head to the Home page to see it live.

How to Take Screenshots: A Refresher

A refresher course for our PC and Mac Users:

PC

Snipping Tool

Read more here
  1. To open the Snipping Tool, select Start, enter snipping tool, then select it from the results.
  2. Select New to take a screenshot.
  3. Rectangular mode is selected by default. You can change the shape of your snip by choosing one of the following options in the toolbar: Rectangular mode, Window mode, Full-screen mode, and Free-form mode.
  4. The screenshot is automatically copied to your clipboard. A notification also appears once you're finished taking a screenshot, select it for more editing options..

Mac

Snipping Tool

Read more here
  1. Press Shift-Command-4, then move the crosshair pointer to where you want to start the screenshot.
  2. Press the mouse or trackpad button, drag over the area you want to capture, then release the mouse or trackpad button.
  3. The screenshot should be saved to your default location, which is usually Desktop or Documents/Downloads.
  4. Screenshots are saved as .png files. Filenames begin with “Screenshot” and include the date and time.

Tips for Playing the Game

Take em or leave em

  1. Create a folder for your screenshots to dump to on your local machine, this will make it easier to upload from the same folder.
  2. Open multiple prompts in different tabs for Uploading a prompt and as you find them, just go back to your tab.
  3. Each prompt is worth different amounts of points so the bottom or the top prompts are not necessarily worst the most or least.
  4. While this game is optimized for desktop viewing, you can play on your phone. If so, be sure to rotate sideways to landscape for the best experience.
